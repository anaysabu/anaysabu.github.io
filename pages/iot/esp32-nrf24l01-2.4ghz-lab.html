2.4 GHz Wireless Resilience Testing Platform

ESP32 + NRF24L01 based lab for studying protocol disruption, packet flooding, and wireless behavior in controlled environments.

Project Overview

This project demonstrates how low-cost microcontrollers like the ESP32, combined with NRF24L01 RF modules, can be used to analyze and stress-test 2.4 GHz wireless protocols. The setup highlights how seemingly simple hardware can influence wireless reliability, interference tolerance, and protocol robustness.

Hardware Gallery

Research Capabilities

  • 2.4 GHz protocol disruption and interference analysis
  • Packet flooding and collision behavior studies
  • Bluetooth and Wi-Fi coexistence observations
  • Understanding deauthentication and channel congestion effects
  • Wireless resilience and mitigation testing

Legal & Ethical Notice

Wireless jamming and intentional interference with communication systems is illegal in many countries and strictly regulated by law.

This project is intended only for educational, research, and defensive testing in controlled environments such as RF-shielded labs or with explicit authorization.

The author does not encourage or support unauthorized use, disruption of public networks, or violation of local wireless regulations.
